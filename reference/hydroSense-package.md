# hydroSense: Water Quality Analysis Tools for Leachate-Impacted Waters

Tools for detecting and assessing landfill-leachate impacts on
freshwater quality, organised around three capabilities. First,
end-member mixing analysis estimates the leachate-mixing fraction
('LMF') of a sample from its major-ion signature, distinguishing
leachate influence from natural variation. Second, a Bayesian
multivariate model imputes below-detection and missing analyte
concentrations while preserving cross-analyte correlation structure.
Third, species-sensitivity- distribution ('SSD') based
potentially-affected-fraction ('PAF') toxicity assessment, including a
multi-substance PAF ('msPAF') that combines toxicants by concentration
and response addition and subtracts local background via the Added Risk
Approach. Toxicity is derived from the ANZECC/ANZG freshwater guideline
datasets, with bioavailability and physicochemical normalisations
(hardness, pH, dissolved organic carbon and temperature) applied so that
samples are compared on a common index-condition basis.
